# Differences between TTRPGs and Survival Horror Video Games

This is an incomplete list of things that I've thought about, in terms of what needs to change to adapt the ideas of survival horror to a tabletop roleplaying game.

## TTRPGs don't have save files

The expectation in survival horror video games is that death is a fail state you will enter multiple times. Games will often find ways to make this feel bad for the player -- extended dying scenes, grisly gameover scenes, and losing progress/resources -- because they are trying to keep dying feeling meaningful despite how often it happens. TTRPG play culture is largely that, absent divine intervention (which is admittedly far easier to come by in D&D than it is in real life), when you're dead you're dead.

So we need to be less lethal than the video games, which means we need to find ways to keep tension even without repeated character deaths.
